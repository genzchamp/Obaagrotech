Newcastle Disease in Poultry: Why It Keeps Coming Back & Natural Plant Solutions That Work

Understanding Newcastle Disease (NDV)

Newcastle Disease is one of the most devastating viral diseases affecting poultry worldwide. It spreads rapidly and can cause high mortality rates if not properly managed. Many farmers continue to lose birds because they only treat symptoms instead of addressing root causes and building natural immunity.

Common Causes Farmers Often Ignore

  • No quarantine for new birds — Introducing new chickens without isolation is the #1 way the virus enters farms.
  • Poor ventilation and overcrowding — Stressed birds have weaker immune systems.
  • Dirty drinking water and wet litter — Creates perfect conditions for the virus to survive.
  • Contact with wild birds — Pigeons, sparrows, and other wild birds can carry the virus.
  • Inconsistent biosecurity — Visitors, shared equipment, and poor foot dips.

Natural Plant Solutions That Support Immunity

1. Aloe Vera

Contains acemannan, a powerful polysaccharide that stimulates the immune system and increases antibody production against Newcastle virus.

How to use: Mix 1–2 teaspoons of fresh aloe vera gel per litre of drinking water, 3 times a week.

2. Marigold (Tagetes)

Has strong antiviral and anti-parasitic properties thanks to flavonoids and thiophenes.

How to use: Plant around the coop or dry flowers and mix small amounts into feed.

3. Garlic & Ginger

Strong natural antiviral and anti-inflammatory effects. Garlic contains allicin which helps fight infections.

How to use: Crush fresh garlic or ginger and add small amounts to drinking water or feed.

4. Neem Leaves

Excellent natural disinfectant for the coop and has antiviral properties.

How to use: Boil neem leaves and use the cooled water for cleaning the coop or as a spray.

Best Prevention Strategy

The most effective approach is Good Biosecurity + Consistent Natural Support:

  • Strict quarantine for all new birds (minimum 2–3 weeks)
  • Improve ventilation and reduce overcrowding
  • Keep litter dry and water clean
  • Use the plants above regularly to boost immunity
  • Vaccinate only when necessary and under proper guidance
